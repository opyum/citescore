The 12 signals that decide whether AI cites your site

When someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI Overview “what's a good tool for X,” the AI picks from sites it can read, trust, and quote. This is GEO/AEO — the SEO of AI search. Run your site against these 12. Most sites fail 7.

Access & crawlability
1

AI bots allowed in robots.txt

If GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot or Google-Extended are blocked, you're invisible to those engines no matter how good your content is.

2

You have an llms.txt file

A simple /llms.txt tells AI models what your site is and how to summarize you. Almost nobody has one — free differentiation right now.

3

Pages render without JavaScript

Many AI crawlers don't run JS well. If key content only appears after a client-side render, the model sees an empty page.

Structure & machine-readability
4

Schema.org structured data

Organization, Product, FAQPage, Article markup tells the AI exactly what you are — in a format it trusts over prose.

5

Answer-first content structure

Lead with the direct answer in the first 1–2 sentences. AI extracts the top of a section; bury the answer and it can't quote you.

6

Clear, descriptive headings

AI uses your heading hierarchy to understand and chunk the page. Vague headings (“Welcome!”) give it nothing to grab.

7

FAQ coverage of real buyer questions

Pages answering “how much does X cost,” “is X better than Y,” “does X do Z” get cited — that's literally what users ask the AI.

Trust & authority
8

Explicit comparison / “vs” content

If you don't state how you compare to alternatives, the AI fills the gap with competitors. Own the comparison.

9

Named author, dates, entity consistency

Consistent name/brand across site, social and the web builds the “entity” the AI recognizes and trusts.

10

External mentions & citations

Being referenced on other credible sites (directories, roundups, forums) is a major trust signal. AI weights what others say about you.

Freshness & coverage
11

Visible freshness signals

Recent “last updated” dates and current info. Stale pages get deprioritized; AI prefers sources that look maintained.

12

Topical depth, not just a landing page

One marketing page isn't enough. A handful of genuinely useful pages gives the AI surface area to cite.

Score yourself: 10–12 = ahead of ~95% of sites · 7–9 = solid, fixable gaps · below 7 = AI is probably recommending your competitors right now, and you'd never know.
